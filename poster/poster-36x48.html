II. Core Algorithm Modules (Module 1)

Module 1: Path Deviation Ratio (Spatial Feasibility)
Academic Concept: Space-Time Prism Constraint
Calculation: For a scheduled route A→B, with pickup P and delivery D:
Δ = (Dist(A,P) + Dist(P,D) + Dist(D,B) - Dist(A,B)) / Dist(A,B)
Threshold: Δ ≤ 15% (Ensures "Zero Marginal Cost" for the Bringer)

Algorithm Section (Part II)

Module 2: Dynamic ETA Estimation (Temporal Modeling)
Academic Concept: Queuing Theory & Dynamic Traffic Assignment
ETA = (Distance / Speed) + T_pickup + P_density
Correction FactorsDetails
Class Schedule DrivenSpeed reduced by 30-50% near academic buildings 5-15 mins after class ends (via Campus API).
Pickup LatencyHistorical wait times at campus stores (Avg. 4 mins peak / 2 mins off-peak).
Building TransitionFixed time penalties for door-opening, ID swiping, and vertical movement (elevators).
Module 3: Unified Indoor-Outdoor Routing (Context-Awareness)
Academic Concept: Multi-layer Weighted Graph Navigation
Modeling Strategy: The campus is modeled as a Multi-layer Weighted Graph where edges have attributes: indoor, semi-indoor, and outdoor.
Adaptive Mechanism: When Temperature < -10°C or Snowing: outdoor weight × 1.5 (Penalty) | indoor weight × 0.8 (Incentive).
Significance: Specifically optimized for the Minnesota climate (Skyways/Tunnels), prioritizing user comfort and mobility accessibility.

IV. Summary Statement

MavSide’s engine transforms "on-the-way" intent into computable spatiotemporal constraints. By integrating deviation thresholds, dynamic ETA correction, and unified indoor-outdoor modeling, it ensures every match is a seamless, low-impact reuse of existing campus mobility.

Theory Section — Core Mechanisms & Framework

I. Secondary Economy & Asset Activation
💰
Secondary Economy
Refers to a derivative economic system generated by capturing the residual value of existing production or consumption activities (such as daily commuting).
→ MavSide transforms students' inevitable displacements (Sunk Travel Costs) into tradable logistics resources, achieving efficient allocation of existing campus capacity.
🪙
Zero Marginal Cost Tendency
A phenomenon where the incremental cost of providing an additional service is extremely low or approaches zero.
→ When a Bringer’s delivery path highly overlaps with their planned route, the delivery incurs near-zero additional time or physical cost, making micro-transaction mutual aid economically viable.
📍
Proximity-based Efficiency
The utilization of extreme geographical closeness to eliminate logistical intermediaries, enabling instant, minute-level responsiveness.
→ Leveraging the geographical boundaries of a closed campus, MavSide ensures that task initiation and fulfillment occur within walking distance, guaranteeing extreme punctuality and efficiency.
II. Circular Economy & Inclusive Growth
♻️
Circular Economy
A model emphasizing the efficient circulation of resources and community mutual aid within a closed-loop system to reduce external dependence and enhance systemic resilience.
→ By establishing a "Peer Trust Network" via mandatory mnsu.edu authentication, MavSide ensures all participants are verified students, utilizing the natural barriers of a closed community to isolate external commercial risks and safeguard a pure and secure transaction environment.
💸
Cross-Subsidization
A pricing strategy that uses profits from mainstream business operations to subsidize specific public welfare or vulnerable group services.
→ Commissions extracted from commercial orders are injected into a public welfare fund, providing long-term shipping fee waivers for Students with Disabilities, achieving precise feedback from economic gains to campus welfare.
👥
Labor Fragmentation & Hyper-Flexibility
A state where labor can instantaneously switch work modes based on individual availability, free from the constraints of fixed scheduling or formal employment.
→ MavSide provides "zero-threshold, zero-constraint" flexible income opportunities for international students with language barriers or fragmented schedules, building a decentralized and highly inclusive campus economic ecosystem.
III. Synchronized Dispatching in Hybrid Spaces
🗺️
Unified Indoor-Outdoor Modeling
A modeling technique that abstracts indoor corridors, tunnels, and outdoor paths into a complex Multi-layer Weighted Graph.
→ Specifically designed for the extreme Minnesota climate, MavSide integrates the campus Skyway and Tunnel systems into its algorithmic road network, enabling precise, all-weather navigation.
⚙️
Context-Aware Algorithm
An intelligent decision-making model that dynamically adjusts path weights based on external environmental variables (e.g., temperature, snowfall).
→ During extreme cold, the algorithm automatically increases the weight of indoor paths, guiding Bringers through indoor routes to achieve the optimal balance between delivery efficiency and personal comfort.
